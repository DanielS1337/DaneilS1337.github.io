Appreciating HIDDEN TALENT

  • by Daniel Schmidt
  • June 30, 2020

Reckoning

  • Passion
  • Personal

I recently got back from a trip to Colorado, which was a much needed break from the bland daily routine I've had the past few weeks. I shot plenty of video, pictures, and even got to use the Super8 camera (I can't wait to get it developed). My flight from Houston landed in Denver early in the morning so my friend Stephen and I got some breakfast and headed off to explore. The first order of business was to go visit a friend, who showed us an amazing trail to drive on. I probably shot half my film roll on the way up, so I hope it looks as nice as I remember. Now as the day passed and the night slipped over the sky, thatâ€™s when this story takes place.

Wandering around the streets of Denver at midnight with Stephen and taking some neat pictures was already cool enough. After a few hours of wandering we were crossing the street back to our hotel, when we were approached by a homeless man who asked if he could perform for us in exchange for some money. Normally this is when I'd walk away but I thought "Hell, why not?" and agreed to the mans offer. The man started beatboxing and wasn't too bad at it. After a couple minutes of this, the two main characters of this story show up. One tall sporting a dope afro with bleached tips. The other, looking like he was from the year 2040, wearing John Lennon glasses, a red button up, and wearing a cheetah-print facemask.

Stephen I quickly made friends with these two guys and it didn't take long for us to find something to do. The guy with the John Lennon glasses was carrying around a guitar and since we were in a relatively empty downtown Denver (thanks COVID) I decided it would be a great opportunity to film. Much to Stephen's and my surprise, not only was this man a great singer and guitarplayer, he wrote his own songs too. Between varying shots at different downtown locations we sat and the four of us talked about life, the taler man read some poetry he had written that hit harder than a freight train, and all of us moved deeper into conversation. As the night went on we got closer and closer, knowing more about each other and what brought us to where we are now. Unfortunately all good things must come to an end and we walked back to our hotel. The feeling I had walking back is still very vivid in my mind, a sort of awe and inspiration.

I'm not really sure what you should take away from this story but what I got from it is this. Talent is hidding everywhere. As a videographer, it's my job to find it, film it, and let the whole world see it's wonder.
