Flow State

  • by Daniel Schmidt
  • October 07, 2020

I have some very fond memories of the airport. As some of you may know I used to work at an airport. I was a line service technician and did a lot of work on military jets and aircraft belonging to NASA. At that same airport is where I got my first official video job, and was tasked with making an internet ad for a maintenance company. I've come a long way since then and I learned a lot working that job, including how to work with deadlines and meet them.

I'd like to share some of the video-editing tips I've picked up to speed up the process and help you meet deadlines. First off, music. If I'm editing, I'm always listening to music until I have to listen to a project's audio. The goal of this is to reach flow state. "In positive psychology, a flow state, also known colloquially as being in the zone, is the mental state in which a person performing some activity is fully immersed in a feeling of energized focus". Playing music while I work is one of the quickest ways for me to enter this "flow state". Normally I will listen to some Lo-Fi to get me started, and then transition into some alt-rock but it varies based on what I'm working on. The point is get yourself a playlist or song that gets you "in the zone" and get moving.

Secondly, sleep. As anyone in the video industry knows, some nights, sleep is a luxury we can't afford. The problem with this is a well rested mind and body is a creative one. Something I've found useful is to set small reminders for yourself throughout the day, and to be consistent with your daily schedule. This way you can reduce having to cram in any last minute work when you should be sleeping. Consistency is key, it doesn't matter what time you go to bed, as long as you do it consistently. I prefer being up late to edit, so I set a time that I need to be asleep by and try to stick to it. Unless it's crucial, it can wait until tomorrow when you're rested and can do your best work.

Lastly, learn keyboard shortcuts for editing. It's a night and day difference between clicking a button 5 times, or just using one shortcut. Not only will it save you time editing, I've found it can make your work better too. Remember that flow state I was just talking about? Not only does it make you work faster, it makes your work nicer and more creative too. The problem with flow state is that it is fleeting. Use it while you have it. Using keyboard shortcuts means more of your work will get done while in flow state.

Hope you found this helpful, thanks for reading and have a nice day!
