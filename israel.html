Jerusalem Syndrome

  • by Daniel Schmidt
  • October 05, 2020

Reckoning

  • Passion
  • Personal

"Jerusalem syndrome is a group of mental phenomena involving the presence of religiously-themed obsessive ideas, delusions, or other psychosis-like experiences that are triggered by a visit to the city of Jerusalem."

Upon landing in Israel and getting on the bus headed for Jerusalem I was warned of this fabled "syndrome". Now I myself didn't experience any crazy religeous outbreaks, but that's not to say the city didn't have it's power. For me it was less of a religeous energy and rather one of inspiration and willingness to share something, to share what I was seeing. Just looking around at the beautiful architecture made me want to post all I could to social media. Luckily though, I was able to overcome my addiction to Instagram and make something worth viewing.

I think my favorite project to date was the video I shot in Israel. I've only published one of the videos, and will publish the others soon, but the one I've published does a good job of relaying my feelings from the trip. Often times (especially in quarantine) I'll find myself bored and reflecting on different exoeriences in my life, and among the most recurring memories lies the Israel trip I made just over a year ago.

Out of all the videos I've made, figuring out the script and which way to take the story took longer on than any other video I've made. Maybe it was because of the emotional gravity it had on me at the time of writing the script. Another factor may have been that this was my first time narrating over a video, and I was trying to avoid explaining what was happening on screen, the video itself should do that, and focus on verbally conveying a different element to really enrich the overall film.

Video aside, this really was a trip for the history books. I was fortunate enough to have been surrounded by wonderful people who I still have contact with to this day. Sitting in the tour bus goofing off, going to lovely bars in Jerusalem, silently sitting in the waters of Tel Aviv watching the waves dance and reflect the moonlight onto a dimly lit beach. Israel made a beautiful video, but the people around me made a beautiful story.
