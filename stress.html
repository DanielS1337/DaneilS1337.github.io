Hard Work and Stress

  • by Daniel Schmidt
  • October 06, 2020

Stress

  • Passion
  • Personal

Let me start this off by saying that I'm okay. I know, or at least think that I'm strong enough to deal with the whole I've dug for myself. 2020 has been bad for a lot of people and I'm blessed to not be 6ft under. That being said I think that the thing that has made me gradually more depressed by the day during 2020 has been the feeling of being lost. I've always been good at dealing with stress, but as the year has gone on, I've been needing that ability more and more. Reflecting back on it, I've become increasingly lost as the days go by.

As a kid I was always smart enough to get past obstacles I faced. I didn't have to work as hard as my peers, I could just "float" through life. I'm realizing now, floating isn't thriving. I've come to the realization that it has caught up to me. I've lost a lot during 2020, I've lost love, I've lost a car I had just bought, I've lost good friends. One after the other they've been chipping away at me, and I've always had the mindset that I would bounce back. But here's the thing, as I'm sure many of you already know, there comes a point where luck and ability don't cut it anymore. Hard work does, and in the end, that's all that counts. Looking around at my peers, I see the success they are starting to reap from their hard work, and I don't feel jealous, I feel inadequacy, regret. Regret that I didn't put myself in a position to find success at this moment as they have. If you put yourself out there and work hard, success will find you.

There is a difference in stressing over if you can pay rent, or stressing about choosing the better of two jobs. You don't get to run from stress, but the decisions you make change which types of stress you feel. "If you shoot for the moon, even if you miss you'll land among the stars." Never did I think I'd be writing a blog post about a quote on a poster in my elementary school. Had I made better choices in the past, I could be an engineering intern right now, telling you about how happy I am that I snagged a high profile internship. How happy I was getting to tell my father the news and watching his face light up in a proud joy. But I made my bed, now I lie in it.

Life is unpredictable, as I was writing this post I got an offer to work with a company that can help my situation a lot, financially and with experience I can take on to future jobs. Will it be the best thing to happen to me this year? Maybe, maybe not. It's not about that. It's about trying. If this isn't the opportunity for me, maybe the next one is.

I'd like to think that I have learned, and that solidifying my thoughts into written words will make sure I never stray off the beaten path again. Only time will tell what I make of myself, and only my own effort will determine that. If you can take anything away from this, know that if you are young and lost, there is a path for you, but only hard work will reveal it to you.
